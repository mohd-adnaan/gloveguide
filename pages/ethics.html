Ethics and Testing

REB Ethics Information

Project Title: Glove Guide

Group G: Kevin Wu, Mohammad Adnaan, William Goyens

Project Description

This project proposes the design of navigation gloves for cyclists and motorcyclists that use vibration feedback to deliver turn-by-turn directions without relying on visual or auditory cues. By embedding small vibration motors into biking gloves and connecting them with a map application, cyclists can receive intuitive left or right turn signals while keeping their focus on the road. This approach reduces distraction, improves safety, and leverages equipment cyclists are already likely to use.

Risk Assessment

Potential Risk: The risks are the same as those typically associated with biking. Participants could fall off their bike or encounter other biking-related hazards during testing.

Risk Mitigation

Safety Measures: Recruit only experienced cyclists as test participants to minimize risk and ensure participants are comfortable with standard cycling conditions.

Safety Considerations

  • Comprehensive safety briefing for all participants before testing begins
  • Testing in controlled environments initially before progressing to real-world scenarios
  • Regular feedback collection for continuous system improvement
  • Emergency protocols in place during all testing sessions
  • Participants must wear appropriate safety equipment (helmet, etc.)
  • Testing limited to experienced cyclists familiar with road safety

Data Privacy

  • Secure handling of all participant information
  • Anonymous data collection methods to protect identity
  • Clear consent procedures before participation
  • Transparent data usage policies communicated to all participants
  • Right to withdraw from the study at any time
  • Data stored securely and deleted after project completion

Ethical Commitment

Our team is committed to conducting all testing and research activities in accordance with McGill University's Research Ethics Board (REB) guidelines. We prioritize participant safety, informed consent, and data privacy throughout the entire project lifecycle. All participants will be fully informed of the study's purpose, potential risks, and their rights as research subjects.
